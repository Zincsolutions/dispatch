# Dispatch — Product Overview

## What is Dispatch?

Dispatch is a centralized AI operations platform and secure vault where companies store, manage, and scale their AI intelligence.

It serves as the system of record for:
- Prompts and prompt frameworks
- Context (company knowledge, tone, instructions, and data)
- AI agents and assistants
- Workflows and repeatable processes
- Tools and platforms used across the organization

Dispatch transforms fragmented AI usage into a structured, secure, and compounding system—where what works is captured, improved, and shared across teams.

At its core, Dispatch is both:
→ An operational layer for AI  
→ A vault for a company’s most valuable AI-driven intellectual property

---

## Who is it for?

Dispatch is designed for:

### Primary Audience
- Small to mid-sized companies (10–200 employees)
- Teams actively using AI but lacking structure, consistency, and security
- Organizations working with partners (like ZINC) to implement AI systems

### Key Users
- Founders / Executives → want visibility, leverage, and protection of AI-driven IP  
- Marketing Teams → using AI for content, campaigns, and brand systems  
- Operations Teams → building workflows and internal efficiencies  
- Individual Contributors → actively using tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney, etc.

---

## What problem does it solve?

### 1. AI Usage is Fragmented
AI work is happening across:
- Personal chat threads
- Slack conversations
- Documents that are never reused
- Individual experiments that don’t scale

There is no central system.

---

### 2. Context is Not Shared
The most important input to AI is not just prompts—it’s context:
- Brand voice
- Business rules
- Customer understanding
- Internal knowledge

Today, this context is:
- Inconsistent
- Rewritten repeatedly
- Not standardized across teams or tools

There is no shared intelligence layer.

---

### 3. Agents and Workflows Are Isolated
Companies are beginning to build:
- Custom GPTs
- Claude projects
- Automated workflows

But:
- They are created in silos
- They are not documented or reusable
- No one knows what exists or what works

There is no orchestration layer.

---

### 4. High-Value Work is Lost
- Effective prompts are not saved or reused  
- Context is not preserved  
- Agent configurations are not shared  
- Improvements are not distributed  

There is no memory.

---

### 5. No Visibility Into What Works
- No signal on which prompts, agents, or workflows perform best  
- No way to identify top-performing systems  
- No way to standardize best practices  

There is no performance layer.

---

### 6. No Security or Ownership of AI IP
A company’s AI assets are quickly becoming a competitive advantage:
- Prompt frameworks  
- Context systems  
- Agent configurations  
- Workflow playbooks  

Yet today, they are:
- Exposed in chat tools  
- Stored on individual machines  
- Easy to lose or access improperly  

There is no vault.

---

## What makes Dispatch different?

### vs. Notion
Notion is a flexible documentation tool.

- Notion stores information  
- Dispatch structures and operationalizes AI systems  

Dispatch introduces:
- Structured layers for prompts, context, and agents  
- Purpose-built organization of AI assets  
- Version-aware systems (future-ready)  
- Clear separation between types of AI assets  

Notion becomes messy and passive.  
Dispatch is structured, intentional, and built for AI.

---

### vs. Slack
Slack is a communication layer.

- Slack enables conversation  
- Dispatch enables accumulation, refinement, and protection  

In Slack:
- Prompts get buried in threads  
- Context is fragmented  
- Knowledge is temporary  

In Dispatch:
- High-value assets are captured and refined  
- Context is standardized  
- AI systems improve over time  

Slack is real-time noise.  
Dispatch is long-term intelligence.

---

### vs. ChatGPT Teams / Claude Teams
These platforms provide shared access to AI—but not structure.

- They enable usage  
- Dispatch enables systemization  

Limitations:
- No centralized context layer  
- No shared agent registry  
- No structured prompt management  
- No cross-team visibility  
- No ownership or protection of AI assets  

Dispatch sits above all tools as the orchestration and control layer.
